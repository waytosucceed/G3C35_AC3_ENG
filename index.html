Future Perfect Tense

The future perfect tense is used to talk about something that will be finished or completed before another time in the future. It helps us know that something will happen before a certain point in the future.

Rule: Subject + Helping verbs (will + have) + Past participle form of the main verb + the rest of the sentence.

Examples:
  • By next week, I will have finished reading this book.
  • They will have completed their homework before dinner.
  • By the time the movie starts, we will have arrived at the theatre.

    • These sentences show that certain actions will be completed before a specified time in the future.

    Simple Future Tense:
    The simple future tense is used to describe actions that will happen at a specific time in the future. It is often used to make predictions, express intentions, or indicate scheduled events.

    Rule: Subject + will + base form of the verb + rest of the sentence.

    Examples:
    "It will rain tomorrow."
    "I will study for the exam tonight."
    Future Continuous Tense
    The future continuous tense describes actions that will be ongoing at a specific point in the future. It is used to indicate that an action will be in progress at a certain time.

    Rule: Subject + will be + present participle (verb + ing) + rest of the sentence.

    Examples:
    "At 8 PM tonight, I will be watching my favourite show."
    "When you arrive, she will be studying in the library."
